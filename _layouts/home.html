We work in dynamic process modelling, model-based control, and machine learning, developing methods that link data and physics to improve real-world systems. Our research spans mineral processing and emerging decarbonisation technologies, focusing on how hybrid models and digital twins can enable real-time prediction, optimisation, and control. These tools are key to advancing the efficiency and sustainability of industrial processes that drive the global energy transition.

Research Themes

Hybrid Modelling

Integration of physics-based and machine-learning modelling for dynamic processes with reliable prediction with quantified uncertainty.

Digital Twins

Real-time virtual replicas for monitoring, fault/anomaly detection, what-if analysis, and predictive control of complex engineering systems.

Advanced Process Control

Nonlinear, stochastic, and economic MPC; state/parameter estimation; and adaptive strategies for safe, optimal operation under uncertainty.

Data-driven Optimisation

Reinforcement learning, Gaussian-process surrogates, and Bayesian optimisation for decision-making.

Smart Minerals & Manufacturing

Model-based optimisation for froth flotation, heap leaching, and SAG mills towards sustainability and productivity.

Carbon Capture & Energy Systems

Dynamic modelling and control for DAC/CCS, solvent regeneration, and hydrogen value chains to improve efficiency and operability.

Software

Bubble Analyser is a comprehensive open-source app that processes images of bubbles, enabling users to quantify bubble size distribution. The tool is free to use and modify under the GNU General Public License, making it accessible to everyone.

Developed in collaboration with Imperial College London and IntelliSense.io, Bubble Analyser was designed with expandability and an easy-to-use interface in mind. It is also available as an executable version for easy deployment.

With Bubble Analyser, you can efficiently analyse bubble images and extract quantitative insights into complex processes. Visit BubbleAnalyser.com ‚Üí
